Objective of this section is to smoothen team management to improve site operations day by day and to encourage a competent, involved and driven team. The chapter has following chapters:


ORGANIZATION STRUCTURE & RECRUITMENT

Success of Taj is based on selecting the right people for the right job which has enabled us in attaining and upholding the standards we offer to our valued customers.

ORGANOGRAM

STATION ORGANOGRAM (STANDARDIZED):

A well defined organization structure with clear roles & responsibilities is the key to success of any business. This is all more important in our industry which has a high customer interface, multiple and recurring operational tasks, and high dependence on people.

Taj Gasoline has created a well-crafted Retail Station organization structure enables a high performing site team. This structure is variable depending on location, products being offered and facilities. The organization structure is the guiding principle defining the (OLE) (Optimum level of employment).


STAFFING:

How to plan resources & set organogram?

Optimum Level of Employment (OLE) is to be planned in consideration of
- Location of the station & nature of customers
- Number & nature of facilities available
- Number of Dispensers at each product
- Forecasted volume of the site
- Expected traffic flow & rush hour


MINIMUM OLE IS GIVEN AS BELOW

Each shift must not excelled 12 Hours

RECRUITMENT

Recruitment is the process of actively seeking out & hiring candidate for a specific position. At Taj Gasoline, OLE (Optimum Level of Employment) set prior to launch of Taj Gasoline Retail Station will be the basis for recruitment. Station will have staffing as per approved headcount as given in the OLE (Any deviation in headcount will be prior approved through respective Taj Gasoline Area Manager. All staffing requirement needs to consider the following:

Peak Hours:

Peak hours can range from city to city and route to route, just as they might between different products being supplied at a station. Adequate staff should be available to cater to Rush Hour demand. There should not be any break (lunch, etc) in Rush Hour except Namaz Break.

Top Seasons of business:

Due to accessibility in terms of commodities movement, there are several regions where summer are busier on the highways than winter. Family travel is more common during summer vacations or in specific winter regions when there is greater traffic. Whereas each area has its own agricultural seasons which will determine requirement for additional staff.

Unusual Absence of Staff:

Retail business has a very high turn-over. Typically, frequent leaves are experienced and need to be catered to.

Approved leaves:

Leaves such as pilgrimage, annual or any other event should be planned to ensure no gaps are there at forecourt or dispensers which ensure smooth handling of customer flow with uninterrupted/delayed service.

Shift Management:

It is used to manage different shift types and help the station create an easy-to-follow schedule for personnel shifts. This is to guarantee that customers receive excellent service with a smile, which is only possible if they are not worn out by arbitrary scheduling.

Placement of Skilled and experienced staff:

During interviews it is evident and can be judged by the Retailer/Taj Gasoline that a skilled staff can be better accommodated where the load is predicted to be extensive resulting in better service. However, during training further filtering of individuals based on their performance should be made.

SHIFT MANAGEMENT:

In order to ensure that all the pieces of the puzzle are in place to enable seamless operational activity for a specified shift, shift management focuses on the large picture by putting all the daily activities of the Retail Station together.

As the Retailer, you will utilize the resources and management skills to coordinate customer service excellence and operations across all areas as you align and engage the shift to run the day. It's crucial that Site Manager and the team communicates among themselves and the Retailer and Area Manager as all are linked with the common goal of service excellence with profitability.

Site Manager is in charge of all shifts and the outcome of each shift both in terms of operational performance, customer service and revenue. This can be only achieved by maintaining the standards set by Taj Gasoline and following through with Shift Management guidelines.

Recommended Shift for Staff at Retail Station should not be greater than 12 Hours.

ATTENDANCE:

Ensuring complete staff attendance on time is very critical since our OLE is defined at optimal level to ensure right customer experience. Given this is a Retail business which is heavily dependent on staff, absence of even one staff member can severely hamper site operations.

Effective monitoring of the attendance is also important to ensure that payroll is processed accurately and there is no fraudulent activity which may impact your profitability.

In order to do so it is mandatory for the Retailer to install the Electronic Attendance Machine (EAS) to ensure proper attendance is taken while entering & exiting the fuel station. Attendance machine is also recommended to avoid fraud at site in form of fake employees, resulting in excessive payroll cost.
Attendance machine can be installed with the help of Taj Gasoline IT team.

DAILY WAGERS

Daily wagers are not recommended by Taj Gasoline given they are not trained resources and may impact customer experience as well as pose a security risk. However, in extreme situations and in order to ensure business continuity, daily Wagers may be hired for maximum of 2 days.

- There is a high turnover in forecourt staff on all retail stations across the industry and Taj Gasoline is no exemption to this. In order to ensure that there is no impact on site operations, the site manager must maintain a record of daily wagers available in the area and those who are already trained.
- Site Manager should manage forecourts through daily wagers only in case of leaves/resign of permanent employee.
- Daily wagers must be utilized in basic activities such as wiper service, traffic management, water boy. - In case a daily wager is appointed as attendant, site manager must impart training on customer service steps & HSSE before commencement of duty.
- It is to be ensured by the site manager to coach the daily wager at forecourt time to ensure optimum and standardize performance.

BREAKS:

Providing breaks during the shift is important to ensure the Staff remains fresh and alert to serve customers.

Breaks should be planned for Prayers and Meal and monitored by Site Manager.

There should not be any break apart from Prayers during the peak traffic timings of the site and staff should remain on the forecourt alert to handle high traffic inflow.

PRAYER BREAKS:

Each attendant will get 15 min break for each Prayer

- Prayers break to be undertaken as per local prayers timing.
- Skelton staff (Cashier & Safety staff) to be remain at 1 x dispensers at forecourt during the Prayer Break to accommodate any drive-in customer.
- Prayer break will start 15 minutes after azan and 5 min before Namaz.
- Site Supervisor will place prayer break board on all fill points 5 minutes before the start of Farz prayers - Staff will offer prayers at the last row of the congregation and should join their area of duties without any delay after the Farz prayers.
- Staff will offer farz prayers together and then will return to their respective posts. Only two staff members at a time can offer Sunnat & Nafl prayers
- Cashier & remaining staff member will only offer prayers after the staff is back, supervisor will be undertaking cashier duties during this time.
- Shift manager to offer Sunnat & Nafl prayers after the entire staff of the statin has offered prayers.
- Site Manager must supervise Prayer Breaks to ensure there is no excessive delay in resumption of fueling.

MEAL BREAKS:

- Site Manager will place meal break schedule on notice board on weekly basis.
- Lunch break is from 1:00 PM to 2:30 PM for a slot of 15 minutes per two persons.
- Dinner break is from 08:30 PM to 10:00 PM for a slot of 15 minutes per two persons.
- Staff members will take permission of meal break from respective supervisor.
- Meal breaks should not be scheduled during Peak Timings

TEA BREAKS:

- Keeping in view the nature of work in the industry we are in, there are no specific tea break allocated. However, tea will be served during shift while at their place of duty. Night shift is served more than day due to their timings.
- Tea should not be served during Peak Timings

EMERGENCY BREAKS:

If any staff member has to leave in emergency due to any specific reason, site manager has to arrange replacement. Site Manager to ensure that this emergency does not occur frequently as it will set a trend for other staff to follow.

It is important for us to ensure that our staff is proud to work with us and is given the due respect. The outlook of the staff plays a very important part in ensuring that the staff is proud of their role and also gives a professional outlook to our valued customers.

STANDARD FOR UNIFORM:


Taj Gasoline has assigned dedicated vendors for development of all uniform’s items according to standard design & material. Uniform must be purchased from designated vendor for which compliance is mandatory without any deviation.

- Two sets of new uniforms are provided to our valuable staff in every season:
  - Summer Season: start of summer months, i.e. April to May each year
  - Winter Season: start of winter months, i.e. October to November each year

- Staff is guided on ensuring uniforms is kept clean and washing guidelines are following:
  - Only use laundry cleaning detergent and do not use soap for cleaning
  - Dry the uniform in shade to avoid fading
  - Use only light ironing

- Order and retain atleast 10% extra uniforms for any staff changes and new joiners

- Check overall grooming and uniforms every day during the shift change briefings

- All staff including Attendants, Guards, Shop Staff, Housekeeping Staff are wearing the standard uniform

UNIFORM HYGIENE:

DON’T’s
- Do not reverse the cap.
- Do not unbutton the shirt.
- Do not lift the collar.
- Do not pull out the shirt.
- Do not wear sandals/chappals (Unless medically advised for a short time)
- Do not fold up the sleeves.

No staff is allowed to work without uniform. This applies on trainee staff also.

PERSONAL HYGIENE:

The cleanliness, demeanor, and behavior of employees are all examples of personal hygiene. For obvious reasons, employers may find that their employees' personal hygiene is a sensitive subject A formal policy makes things less awkward because staff members will know what is expected of them.

We must maintain personal hygiene in all facets of daily life, from dawn to night, in order to safeguard our health. To stay healthy, one must regularly wash their clothes and clean their bodies, hair, mouths, and teeth. Personal cleanliness and health go hand in hand.

Since we frequently engage with customers who are also our guests in the industry we work in, initial impressions are crucial. Staff must maintain their own hygiene and the cleanliness of the premises to ensure a lasting impression on the customer. To keep the personnel and our valued customers safe and healthy, it is crucial to have warning signs posted in case of a pandemic and strict SOPs that must be followed in accordance with regulatory instructions.

Employees should be reminded of the need of maintaining good personal hygiene by explaining the situation to them, treating them with respect, and stressing the implications for interactions with clients and coworkers. Weekly reminders from the station manager are necessary to ensure that everyone on staff complies with the rules strictly. The Retailer/Site Manager must make sure the station manager complies with company policies because it immediately affects his business.
